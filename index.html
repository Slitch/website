BJJ - Brazilian Jiu Jitsu

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ) is a martial art/self-defense system that places great emphasis on floor combat and makes excellent use of joint locks and choking grips. BJJ is a Brazilian version of the Japanese martial art jiu-jitsu.

BJJ first became famous outside of Brazil in 1993 when Royce Gracie won the first edition of the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC). Here he won over 3 opponents on the same night with a total match time of fewer than 5 minutes. Royce Gracie later also won the 2nd and 4th editions of the UFC in the open weight class.

BJJ is known for its effectiveness in so-called "everything is allowed" combat - Vale Tudo in Portuguese, and can be trained both with and without a gi. The gi used in BJJ is of strong quality and very similar to that of judo. Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu arose as a result of the Japanese judo expert Mitsuyo Maeda visiting Brazil in 1914 and spreading his knowledge, which excelled especially in floor fighting. Here he got, among others, Carlos and Hélio Gracie as his students, who later further developed the combat system. Brazilian jiu-jitsu is also known as Gracie Jiu-jitsu because the Gracie family was largely responsible for the spread of BJJ in the western world.

From 2010 until 2018 I (Brian Berggren) represented Grandmaster Flavio Behring Red belt 9th degree from Brazil, which is one of the highest graduates in the world.
